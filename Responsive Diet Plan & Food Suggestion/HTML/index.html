Health food for lunch

For a healthy lunch, consider these options:
Grilled Chicken Salad: Mixed greens with grilled chicken, cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, avocado, and a light vinaigrette. Add nuts or seeds for extra protein and crunch.
Quinoa Bowl: Quinoa with roasted vegetables (like sweet potatoes, bell peppers, and broccoli), chickpeas, and a tahini or lemon dressing.
Vegetable Stir-Fry: Stir-fried mixed vegetables with tofu or shrimp, served over brown rice or cauliflower rice.

Health food for breakfast

For a healthy breakfast, consider these options:
Overnight Oats: Rolled oats soaked in almond milk with chia seeds, topped with fresh berries, nuts, and a drizzle of honey or maple syrup.
Greek Yogurt Parfait: Greek yogurt layered with granola, fresh fruit, and a sprinkle of flaxseeds or chia seeds.

Health food for dinner

For a healthy dinner, consider these options:
Grilled Salmon with Steamed Vegetables: A grilled salmon fillet served with steamed or roasted vegetables like asparagus, broccoli, and carrots, and a side of quinoa or brown rice.
Stuffed Bell Peppers: Bell peppers stuffed with a mixture of lean ground turkey or beef, quinoa or brown rice, black beans, and diced tomatoes, baked until tender.

Health food for dinner

For a healthy dinner, consider these options:
Vegetarian Stir-Fry: A mix of colorful vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, broccoli, and snap peas stir-fried with tofu or tempeh in a light soy or teriyaki sauce, served over brown rice or cauliflower rice.
Baked Chicken with Sweet Potatoes: Baked chicken breast seasoned with herbs, served with roasted sweet potatoes and a side salad of mixed greens.
Lentil and Vegetable Stew: A hearty stew made with lentils, carrots, spinach, tomatoes, and onions, seasoned with herbs and spices, served with a slice of whole-grain bread.
